üîí Access Control Mechanisms

The A.R.I.A. system implements multiple access control mechanisms to ensure secure operation and prevent unauthorized access.

üîê Authentication Methods

UUID-Based Authentication

Primary authentication method using Second Life UUIDs

  • Cryptographically Secure: UUIDs are cryptographically unique identifiers
  • Non-Spoofable: Cannot be forged or impersonated within SL
  • Persistent: UUID remains constant across sessions and locations
  • Validation: System validates UUID format and existence

Role-Based Access Control (RBAC)

Access permissions based on assigned roles rather than individual permissions

  • Hierarchical: Higher roles inherit lower role permissions
  • Granular: Specific permissions for specific functions
  • Centralized: Role definitions managed centrally
  • Auditable: All role assignments and changes logged
