Safety Philosophy

Safety First: The Drone Controller System is designed with safety as the highest priority. Every feature includes multiple layers of protection, and your wellbeing is always more important than any session or activity.

Core Safety Principles

üõ°Ô∏è Defense in Depth

Multiple independent safety systems ensure that if one fails, others will protect you.

  • Hardware-level emergency controls
  • Software-based safety monitoring
  • Manual override capabilities
  • External safety contacts

‚ö° Fail-Safe Design

When systems fail, they default to the safest possible state.

  • Connection loss triggers automatic release
  • System errors result in restriction removal
  • Unknown commands are rejected
  • Emergency functions cannot be disabled

ü§ù Informed Consent

All activities must be based on clear understanding and ongoing consent.

  • Clear communication of risks and benefits
  • Explicit consent for specific activities
  • Right to withdraw consent at any time
  • Regular check-ins and consent verification

üìö Continuous Learning

Safety systems and practices continuously improve based on experience and feedback.

  • Regular safety system updates
  • Community feedback integration
  • Incident analysis and prevention
  • Best practice sharing

Responsibility and Accountability

Your Responsibilities as a Wearer

  • Know Your Limits: Understand and communicate your personal boundaries
  • Maintain Communication: Keep open dialogue with controllers and safety contacts
  • System Maintenance: Keep your HUD updated and functioning properly
  • Emergency Preparedness: Know and practice emergency procedures
  • Honest Feedback: Report safety concerns and incidents

Controller Responsibilities

  • Respect Boundaries: Honor all stated limits and consent requirements
  • Monitor Safety: Continuously watch for signs of distress or system issues
  • Emergency Response: Be prepared to immediately stop sessions if needed
  • Clear Communication: Explain actions and maintain regular check-ins
  • Aftercare: Provide appropriate support after sessions

Manual Override Systems

HUD Detachment

Standard Detachment

Right-click the HUD ‚Üí "Detach" - This completely removes the HUD and all associated restrictions.

Emergency Detachment

If the HUD becomes unresponsive:

  1. Open your viewer's "Worn" inventory tab
  2. Find the Wearer HUD in the list
  3. Right-click and select "Detach"
  4. If that fails, use "Replace Outfit" with a saved outfit

RLV Emergency Procedures

RLV Disable Method
  1. Open viewer Preferences
  2. Navigate to RLV/RLVa settings
  3. Uncheck "Enable RLV"
  4. Restart viewer to ensure complete reset
Clear All Restrictions

Type in local chat: @clear

This removes all active RLV restrictions from any source.

System-Level Emergency Features

Deadman's Switch

The HUD includes a "deadman's switch" that monitors for signs of system failure or user distress:

  • Heartbeat Monitoring: Regular "I'm okay" signals required
  • Response Time Monitoring: Unusual delays trigger safety checks
  • Pattern Recognition: Abnormal interaction patterns detected
  • System Health: Technical failures trigger automatic safety mode

Emergency Contact System

Primary Emergency Contact
  • Trusted person who knows about your drone activities
  • Receives immediate notification of safeword use
  • Has emergency override capabilities if configured
  • Should be available during your session times
Secondary Safety Network
  • Additional contacts for escalated emergencies
  • Professional support resources
  • Community safety network
  • Local emergency services (extreme situations)

  • Remove all active restrictions
  • Activate emergency mode
  • Full emergency contact notification
  • Begin emergency procedures

Emergency Procedures

Emergency Response Hierarchy

üö® Level 1: Immediate Danger

Response Time: Immediate (0-10 seconds)

Situations:
  • Medical emergency or physical distress
  • Complete system failure or malfunction
  • Unwanted or non-consensual activity
  • Loss of consciousness or awareness
Actions:
  1. Immediate: Use safeword or emergency button
  2. Backup: Manually detach HUD
  3. Last Resort: Disable RLV in viewer
  4. Get Help: Contact emergency services if needed

‚ö†Ô∏è Level 2: Safety Concern

Response Time: Very Quick (10-60 seconds)

Situations:
  • Controller becomes unresponsive
  • Unexpected or concerning restrictions
  • System behaving abnormally
  • Feeling uncomfortable or unsafe
Actions:
  1. Communicate: Express concerns immediately
  2. Pause: Request temporary session suspension
  3. Assess: Evaluate safety and comfort level
  4. Decide: Continue with modifications or end session

‚ÑπÔ∏è Level 3: Concern or Question

Response Time: Prompt (1-5 minutes)

Situations:
  • Uncertain about controller's instructions
  • Technical issues or glitches
  • Approaching personal limits
  • Need clarification or break
Actions:
  1. Communicate: Ask questions or express needs
  2. Request: Ask for clarification or modification
  3. Negotiate: Discuss alternatives or adjustments
  4. Continue: Proceed when comfortable

Specific Emergency Scenarios

Scenario 1: Controller Suddenly Disappears

Immediate Actions (First 2 minutes):
  1. Try to contact controller via IM
  2. Check if it's a connection issue
  3. Monitor HUD for automatic safety responses
  4. Prepare for potential emergency action
If No Response (2-5 minutes):
  1. Use safeword to activate emergency release
  2. Contact your emergency contact person
  3. Move to a safe, private location
  4. Document what happened
Follow-up Actions:
  1. Verify all restrictions are completely removed
  2. Check your physical and emotional state
  3. Contact support if technical issues occurred
  4. Debrief with emergency contact

Scenario 2: HUD Malfunction or Unresponsive

Immediate Assessment:
  1. Try touching different parts of the HUD
  2. Check if any buttons still respond
  3. Look for error messages in local chat
  4. Test basic avatar movement and communication
Progressive Response:
  1. First: Try the emergency button multiple times
  2. Second: Type your safeword in local chat
  3. Third: Right-click detach the HUD
  4. Fourth: Use viewer RLV disable if needed
System Recovery:
  1. Restart your viewer completely
  2. Check for any remaining restrictions
  3. Contact technical support with error details
  4. Test HUD functionality before next use

Scenario 3: Unwanted or Excessive Restrictions

Immediate Response:
  1. Clearly communicate "This was not agreed to"
  2. Request immediate removal of unwanted restrictions
  3. If not immediately complied with, use safeword
  4. Document exactly what restrictions were applied
Safety and Legal Considerations:
  • Any activity without consent is inappropriate
  • You have the right to stop any activity immediately
  • Controller should respect limits without argument
  • Consider ending relationship with non-compliant controllers

Safety Best Practices

Pre-Session Safety Planning

Partner Vetting and Communication

Before Your First Session
  • Experience Check: Verify controller's experience and knowledge
  • Reference Check: Talk to others who have worked with them
  • Boundary Discussion: Clearly communicate limits and expectations
  • Safety Knowledge: Ensure they understand emergency procedures
  • Consent Negotiation: Agree on specific activities and boundaries
  • Trial Period: Start with short, low-intensity sessions
Red Flags to Watch For
üö® Critical Warning Signs
  • Pressuring you to skip safety procedures
  • Refusing to discuss limits or boundaries
  • Dismissing your concerns or questions
  • Pushing for immediate intense sessions
  • Unwillingness to provide references
‚ö†Ô∏è Caution Signs
  • Impatience with safety discussions
  • Vague about their experience level
  • Reluctant to answer safety questions
  • Inconsistent communication style
  • Avoiding discussion of aftercare

Session Environment Preparation

Physical Environment
  • Private Space: Use areas where you won't be interrupted
  • Comfortable Setup: Good seating, proper lighting, temperature control
  • Emergency Access: Keep phone and emergency contacts easily accessible
  • Basic Needs: Water, snacks, and comfort items nearby
  • Time Management: Allow sufficient time without rushing
Virtual Environment
  • Stable Connection: Ensure reliable internet connectivity
  • Updated Software: Keep viewer and HUD systems current
  • Backup Communication: Alternative contact methods ready
  • Safe Locations: Pre-selected secure virtual locations
  • Privacy Settings: Appropriate viewer and SL privacy configurations

During-Session Safety Practices

Communication Guidelines

Progressive Safety Approach

Session Intensity Progression
Level 1: Introduction (Sessions 1-3)
  • Basic communication and interface familiarization
  • Simple, non-restrictive commands only
  • Frequent check-ins every 5-10 minutes
  • Maximum 30-minute sessions
  • Focus on trust building and system learning
Level 2: Basic Operations (Sessions 4-10)
  • Introduction of mild restrictions
  • Movement and communication controls
  • Check-ins every 10-15 minutes
  • 45-60 minute sessions
  • Exploration of preferences and boundaries
Level 3: Intermediate Activities (Sessions 11+)
  • More complex restriction combinations
  • Extended duration activities
  • Reduced check-in frequency (based on comfort)
  • 90+ minute sessions
  • Advanced features and customization

Post-Session Safety Practices

Immediate Post-Session Protocol

System Verification (First 5 minutes)
  1. Restriction Check: Verify all restrictions are completely removed
  2. System Status: Confirm HUD shows normal operational status
  3. Avatar Function: Test basic movement, communication, and interface access
  4. Emergency Systems: Verify safeword and emergency button still work
Personal Well-being Check (5-15 minutes)
  1. Physical State: Check for any discomfort, tension, or fatigue
  2. Emotional State: Assess feelings and emotional responses
  3. Mental Clarity: Ensure clear thinking and decision-making ability
  4. Overall Comfort: General sense of safety and satisfaction
Communication and Debrief (15-30 minutes)
  1. Controller Check-in: Discuss session experience with controller
  2. Feedback Exchange: Share what worked well and what didn't
  3. Boundary Review: Adjust limits based on experience
  4. Future Planning: Discuss next session or relationship continuation

Aftercare and Recovery

Physical Aftercare
  • Hydration: Drink water to rehydrate
  • Nutrition: Light snack if session was lengthy
  • Movement: Gentle stretching or walking
  • Rest: Allow time for physical recovery
Emotional Aftercare
  • Processing Time: Allow time to process the experience
  • Support Contact: Reach out to trusted friends if needed
  • Self-Care Activities: Engage in comforting activities
  • Journal/Notes: Record thoughts and feelings about the session
Technical Aftercare
  • System Backup: Save current HUD configuration
  • Log Review: Check session logs for any issues
  • Update Settings: Adjust preferences based on experience
  • Maintenance: Perform any needed system updates

Reporting and Response Systems

Incident Reporting

When to Report
  • Safety Violations: Boundary violations, non-consensual activities
  • Technical Issues: System failures that create safety risks
  • Abuse or Harassment: Any form of abuse, harassment, or coercion
  • Emergency Incidents: Serious safety events or near-misses
How to Report
  1. Immediate Safety: Ensure your immediate safety first
  2. Document: Record details while fresh in memory
  3. Report: Submit to appropriate authorities or community moderators
  4. Support: Seek support from trusted community members
  5. Follow-up: Participate in investigation and resolution process
Report Contents
  • Date, time, and duration of incident
  • Detailed description of what occurred
  • Names and contact information of all involved parties
  • Witnesses or other sources of verification
  • Evidence such as chat logs, screenshots, or system logs
  • Impact on your safety, wellbeing, or others
