Attaching Your HUD

First-Time Attachment

1

Locate Your HUD

Find the Wearer HUD object in your inventory. It should be in the folder where you received it, typically named "Drone Controller - Wearer HUD".

2

Attach to HUD

Right-click the HUD object and select "Attach To" ‚Üí "Attach to HUD" ‚Üí "Top Right" (recommended). You can change the position later if needed.

3

Wait for Initialization

The HUD will automatically start up and initialize its systems. You should see status messages in local chat and the HUD interface will become active.

4

Verify Basic Function

Check that all status indicators are showing and the HUD responds to touches. The power indicator should show green and connection status should show "Standby".

Understanding Your HUD Interface

Status Display Area

  • Power Level: Shows system energy/battery status
  • Connection: Link status with controllers
  • Mode: Current operational state
  • Timer: Session duration when active

Control Buttons

  • EMERGENCY (Red): Immediate safety stop - always accessible
  • Power (Green): Turn HUD on/off
  • Settings (Gray): Access configuration menu
  • Info (Blue): Status information and help

HUD Position Adjustment

If you need to change your HUD's position:

  1. Right-click the HUD and select "Detach"
  2. Right-click the HUD object in inventory again
  3. Choose "Attach To" ‚Üí "Attach to HUD" ‚Üí select your preferred position
  4. Common positions: Top Right, Top Left, Bottom Center, Right Center
Tip: Top Right is recommended as it doesn't interfere with most other HUDs and provides easy access to all controls.

Initial Configuration

Accessing Settings

Click the "Settings" button on your HUD to open the configuration menu. Your HUD comes with safe defaults, but you should customize key personal settings.

Essential Settings to Configure

Personal Information

Display Name

Set how you want to be identified in logs and communications. This can be your SL display name or a preferred identifier.

Emergency Contact

Provide the name or UUID of a trusted person who can be contacted in emergencies. This should be someone who knows about your drone activities.

Safety Configuration

Safeword Setup

Critical: Choose your personal safeword. Default is "EMERGENCY" but you should pick something memorable and unique to you.

Safeword: [YOUR_SAFEWORD_HERE]
Session Limits
  • Maximum Session Duration: Start with 60-90 minutes for first sessions
  • Connection Timeout: Recommended 5-10 minutes
  • Emergency Teleport Location: Set to a safe, private location you control

Communication Preferences

Status Updates
  • Update Frequency: Every 5 minutes (recommended for beginners)
  • Include Location: Enable for safety monitoring
  • Chat Relay: Consider carefully - this forwards your chat to controllers
Notification Preferences
  • Audio Alerts: Enable for important notifications
  • Visual Notifications: Enable for status changes
  • Silent Mode: Available for quiet operation

Quick Configuration Wizard

For first-time users, the HUD includes a configuration wizard. Click "Run Setup Wizard" in the settings menu to be guided through essential settings.

Step 1: Personal safety information (safeword, emergency contact)
Step 2: Session preferences (time limits, communication settings)
Step 3: Interface customization (colors, layout, audio)
Step 4: Testing and verification of all systems

Safety Setup

Mandatory Safety Configuration

Critical: Do not skip this section. Proper safety setup is essential for safe operation.

Safeword Testing

1

Test Chat Safeword

Type your safeword in local chat. The HUD should immediately respond with "Emergency safeword activated" and show emergency status.

2

Test Emergency Button

Click the red EMERGENCY button. It should have the same effect as the safeword and be easily accessible at all times.

3

Test Manual Detach

Practice manually detaching the HUD: right-click ‚Üí "Detach". This is your ultimate backup if other systems fail.

RLV Safety Check

If you have RLV enabled, test the safety systems:

  1. Have the HUD apply a simple restriction (like preventing flying)
  2. Use your safeword to remove all restrictions
  3. Verify that restrictions are completely cleared
  4. Test that you can move and act normally

Starting Your First Session

1

Controller Connection

Work with your controller to establish the connection between your HUDs. You should see your connection status change from "Standby" to "Connected".

2

Initial Communication Test

Verify two-way communication is working. Your controller should be able to send messages and receive your status updates.

3

Simple Test Commands

Start with very simple, non-restrictive commands to verify the system is working correctly. For example, a simple message or status request.

4

Gradual Introduction

If using RLV restrictions, start with very mild limitations and gradually increase complexity as comfort and trust develop.

What to Expect

During Your Session

  • Regular Check-ins: Expect frequent status questions, especially during first sessions
  • Clear Instructions: Good controllers will explain what they're doing and why
  • Respect for Limits: Your stated boundaries should always be honored
  • System Monitoring: Watch your HUD for any warnings or alerts
  • Open Communication: Feel free to ask questions or express concerns

Warning Signs to Watch For

üö® Immediate Concern - Use Safeword
  • Commands or restrictions you didn't agree to
  • Loss of communication with controller
  • System errors or malfunctions
  • Any feeling of being unsafe or uncomfortable
‚ö†Ô∏è Caution - Communicate Immediately
  • Restrictions lasting longer than agreed
  • Unexpected behavior from the system
  • Controller not responding to questions
  • Approaching session time limits

Ending Your Session

Normal Session End

  1. Session Conclusion: Controller initiates proper session end sequence
  2. Restriction Removal: All limitations are gradually and completely removed
  3. System Verification: Confirm your HUD shows normal, unrestricted status
  4. Debrief: Discuss the experience with your controller
  5. Aftercare: Take time for personal care and processing

Post-Session Care

  • Physical Check: Ensure you feel physically comfortable and well
  • Emotional Processing: Take time to process the experience
  • System Status: Verify your HUD returned to normal operation
  • Hydration and Rest: Take care of basic physical needs
  • Communication: Stay in contact with controller for immediate aftercare
